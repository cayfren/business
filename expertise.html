Accounting & Bookkeeping

Managing finances is crucial for any business. Proper accounting ensures compliance, financial clarity, and better decision-making.

  • Keep Accurate Records: Track daily transactions, reconcile bank statements, and manage accounts efficiently.
  • Financial Statements: Maintain Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow reports for insights and compliance.
  • UAE Compliance: Follow IFRS, record VAT properly, and store financial records for at least five years.
  • Payroll Management: Process salaries accurately, comply with WPS, and document employee payments.
  • Be Audit-Ready: Organize financial records, support transactions with documentation, and conduct internal reviews.
  • Use Technology: Automate invoicing, leverage cloud-based accounting, and gain insights with AI analytics.

VAT Compliance

Companies must maintain proper records and meet filing deadlines to ensure VAT compliance.

  • Who Needs to Register? Mandatory for businesses exceeding AED 375,000 turnover, voluntary for those between AED 187,500 - 375,000.
  • VAT Compliance & Filing: Issue invoices correctly, maintain records, file returns on time, and settle VAT payments.

Corporate Tax Compliance

Businesses must understand their corporate tax obligations and ensure compliance.

  • Taxable Businesses: Mainland companies, free zone entities, and foreign businesses with a permanent establishment in the UAE.
  • Exempt Entities: Government bodies, extractive industries (oil & gas), and certain non-resident businesses.
  • Corporate Tax Rates: 0% for profits up to AED 375,000; 9% for profits above AED 375,000.

UAE Free Zone Structuring

Free zones in the UAE offer businesses full foreign ownership, tax benefits, and a streamlined setup process.

  • 100% Foreign Ownership: No local sponsor required.
  • Tax Benefits: Some free zones offer 0% corporate tax and VAT exemptions.
  • Simplified Setup: Quick licensing, reduced bureaucracy, and flexible office options.
